Bob vs AI

Bob just graduated from university and is looking to land his first software engineering job. After a couple weeks of searching, Bob gets an interview request from his dream company! He carefully reads the portion of the email that explains the interview process: 

    You will be given N leetcode-style questions to solve.
    These same questions will be given to M cutting-edge AI models to solve as well.
    To pass the interview, you must solve more questions correctly than each of the AI models.

Given the probabilities that Bob and the AI models will solve each question correctly, help Bob calculate the probability that he will pass the interview.

Input

The first line will contain two integers $N$ and $M$ - the number of questions to solve, and the number of AI models that Bob has to compete against.

The second line will contain N decimal values between $0$ and $1$. The $i$th number is the probability that Bob will solve the $i$th problem correctly.

Next there will be $M$ lines, each containing $N$ decimal values between $0$ and $1$. The $i$th number in the $j$th line is the probability that the $i$th problem will be solved correctly by the $j$th AI model.

$1 \leq N \leq 1000$
$1 \leq M \leq 10$

Output

A single decimal value between $0$ and $1$ - the probability that Bob solves more questions correctly than each of the AI models. Your output must have an absolute or relative error of less than 10^-6 to be considered correct.

Sample Input 1

1 1
0.75
0.5

Sample Output 1

0.375

Sample Input 2

3 2
1 1 1
0.5 0.5 0.5
0.75 0.6 0.5

Sample Output 2

0.678125

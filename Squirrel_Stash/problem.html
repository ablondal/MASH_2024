Squirrel Stash

In the fall, squirrels' brains have been shown to increase in size up to 15%. This is in order to help them remember all the places they've hidden their food away for the winter. Stella is one such squirrel.

In the frosty depths of winter, Stella's silhouette has slimmed.

Springtime had her secretly stashing seeds, smuggling them into spy-proof stores. Since then, snow has shielded the sticks she sorted as signposts for her subsistence stores. As a savvy squirrel, however, she studied a system to spare her the starvation that might stem from this situation.

She summons strings of memory, strides that send her in sequence to each stash where a scrumptious supply securely sleeps. Support Stella's search, so she swiftly snags sustenance from the snow-laden soil!

Her territory is modelled as a two-dimensional plane, and Stella starts at the point (0, 0), pointing East (towards (1, 0)). She remembers a list of actions that she took to hide her food. Each action is either a distance walked straight, a counter-clockwise turn, or a seed stashed. Output the position of each seed she buried, in order.

Input

The first line will contain an integer $n$, indicating the number of actions Stella remembers. $n$ lines follow. Each one consists of one of three possible actions:

  • stride d: Indicates that Stella walked $d$ metres directly forward.
  • spin r: Indicates that Stella turned exactly r degrees counter-clockwise.
  • stash: Indicates that Stella stashed a seed.

$d$ and $r$ are guaranteed to be integers.
$1 \leq n \leq 100$
$1 \leq d \leq 100$
$0 \leq r \leq 359$

Output

Print $n$ lines, one for each of Stella's stash commands. Line $i$ should contain the $x$ and $y$ coordinates of Stella's $i$th stash. Your answer will be considered correct if each number in your answer has absolute or relative error smaller than 10^-3.

Sample Input 1

3
spin 90
stride 1
stash

Sample Output 1

0 1

Hockey Cards (Easy)

Marly, an avid collector of hockey cards, decided to spend her day organizing her extensive collection of $n$ cards. Each card represents a different player and has three attributes, Name, Top Speed, and Height.

Marly has very particular preferences for her cards, such as favoring players whose names start with a certain letter over those with specific height ranges. The preferences are provided in the form of selection criteria, where a selection can be one of:

  • Name a b: All players whose names start with a letter between $a$ and $b$.
  • Speed x y: All players with a top speed between $x$ and $y$.
  • Height x y: All players with a height between $x$ and $y$.
All ranges are inclusive. $a$ and $b$ here would be capital letters, while $x$ and $y$ are integers. It is guaranteed that $a \leq b$ (alphabetically), and $x \leq y$. For instance, Name A C would include players whose names start with A, B, or C.

Your goal is to assist Marly in ordering her cards in a pile from most to least preferred, adhering to her given constraints. If no valid ordering exists, tell her so.

Input

The first line of input holds two integers $n$ and $q$. $n$ is the number of hockey cards that Marly has. $q$ is the number of preferences. The next $n$ lines each denote a hockey card, and contain a string starting with a capital letter (name), and two integers, speed and height respectively. All names are unique.

Following are $q$ lines, each denoting a preference. Preferences are expressed as pairs of selections, as explained above, and are seperated by a greater-than symbol $>$, denoting that all cards in the first selection are preferred over all cards in the second selection.

Output

Output a list of names denoting the pile of cards in any order satisfying Marly's preferences, with her most preferred card on top. If no valid ordering exists, output
your preferences are wrong.

Bounds

$n \leq 1000$.
$q \leq 1000$.
All Speed attributes are between $15$ and $70$.
All Height attributes are between $50$ and $220$.

Sample Input 1

5 3
Alice 30 100
Bob 20 200
Carl 40 150
Derek 50 125
Eve 60 175
Name D E > Speed 20 40
Height 125 175 > Height 100 100
Speed 60 70 > Name A D

Sample Output 1

Eve
Derek
Bob
Carl
Alice

Note that Carl and Alice are interchangeable here!

Sample Input 2

3 3
Alice 30 100
Bob 20 200
Carl 40 150
Name A A > Speed 40 40
Height 140 160 > Name B B
Speed 20 20 > Height 75 125

Sample Output 2

your preferences are wrong

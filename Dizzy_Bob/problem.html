Dizzy Bob

Bob is stuck in an $N$ by $M$ rectangular maze and needs to find a way out. Unfortunately, Bob’s task is made harder by the fact that he gets dizzy extremely easily. In fact, even just a 90 degree turn may cause him to lose his balance and fall down. To reduce the risk of falling down, Bob wants to find a path through the rectangular maze that requires him to change direction the least number of times possible. Bob starts in the top-left corner of the maze, and the exit is at the bottom-right corner of the maze. Bob can only move in 4 directions (up, down, left, right), and can’t pass through any obstacles in his way. What is the minimum number of times he must change direction before reaching the exit?

Input

The first line contains two integers $N$ and $M$ - the height and width of the maze respectively. Next, there will be an $N$ by $M$ grid of characters that shows the structure of the maze. A $#$ represents an obstacle, while a $.$ represents an area that Bob can walk on. It is guaranteed that there are no obstacles in the top left and bottom right corners of the maze.

$1 \leq N \leq 1000$
$1 \leq M \leq 1000$

Output

Output a single integer - the minimum number of times that Bob must change direction to reach the exit. If there is no path that Bob can take to reach the exit, output -1.

Sample Input 1

3 3
...
...
...

Sample Output 1

1

If Bob chooses the path Right-Right-Down-Down, he only needs to change direction 1 time to reach his destination.

Sample Input 2

3 3
.#.
...
.#.

Sample Output 2

2

The best path Bob can take is Down-Right-Right-Down, and he needs to change direction 2 times.

Sample Input 3

3 3
.#.
#..
...

Sample Output 3

-1

Bob can't reach his destination because he is blocked by obstacles.
